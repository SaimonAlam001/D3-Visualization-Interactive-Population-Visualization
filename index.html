What Do These Entities Mean?

This section explains the aggregate entities you can explore in the Entities View:

  • Arab World: Countries in the Middle East and North Africa, defined by the Arab League.
  • Caribbean small states: Island nations in the Caribbean with small populations and economies.
  • Central African Republic: A low-income, landlocked country in Central Africa (also included here as an entity).
  • Central Europe and the Baltics: Countries in Central and Eastern Europe including the Baltics.
  • Early-demographic dividend: Countries with a growing working-age population and declining birth rate.
  • East Asia & Pacific: A World Bank-defined region including China, Indonesia, and other Asia-Pacific countries.
  • East Asia & Pacific (IDA & IBRD countries): Subset of East Asia & Pacific that qualify for World Bank assistance.
  • East Asia & Pacific (excluding high income): Same region as above, excluding developed nations.
  • Euro area: Countries that use the euro as their official currency.
  • Europe & Central Asia: Covers a broad region from Western Europe to Central Asia.
  • Europe & Central Asia (IDA & IBRD countries): Subset that qualifies for development loans/grants.
  • Europe & Central Asia (excluding high income): Same region excluding wealthy nations.
  • European Union: 27 member countries with a shared political and economic union.
  • Fragile and conflict affected situations: Countries with ongoing conflicts or institutional instability.
  • Heavily indebted poor countries (HIPC): Nations identified as needing debt relief from the IMF/World Bank.
  • High income: Countries with gross national income (GNI) per capita over $13,845.
  • IBRD only: Countries that qualify only for IBRD loans (middle-income & credit-worthy low-income).
  • IDA & IBRD total: All countries eligible for either IDA or IBRD programs.
  • IDA blend: Eligible for both IDA grants and IBRD loans due to transitional income status.
  • IDA only: Low-income countries eligible for interest-free loans and grants only from IDA.
  • IDA total: Combination of IDA only and blend countries.
  • Late-demographic dividend: Countries nearing demographic maturity, still benefiting from age shifts.
  • Latin America & Caribbean: Region of countries south of the U.S., including the Caribbean.
  • Latin America & Caribbean (excluding high income): Same region excluding richer countries.
  • Latin America & the Caribbean (IDA & IBRD countries): Subset qualifying for World Bank financial aid.
  • Least developed countries: UN classification: Countries recognized by the UN as having the lowest indicators of development.
  • Low & middle income: All countries with income levels below high-income threshold.
  • Low income: Nations with a GNI per capita of $1,085 or less.
  • Lower middle income: Countries with GNI per capita between $1,086 and $4,255.
  • Middle East & North Africa: Geopolitical region including Egypt, Iran, Morocco, etc.
  • Middle East & North Africa (IDA & IBRD countries): Subset that qualify for World Bank help.
  • Middle East & North Africa (excluding high income): Same region minus rich nations like Qatar or UAE.
  • Middle income: Includes lower and upper middle income groups.
  • North America: Canada, United States, and sometimes Mexico.
  • OECD members: Developed economies part of the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development.
  • Pacific island small states: Tiny island nations in the Pacific region with small populations.
  • Post-demographic dividend: Countries where population aging has started, reducing the economic boost from demographics.
  • Pre-demographic dividend: Countries with high fertility and growing young populations.
  • South Asia: Region including India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.
  • South Asia (IDA & IBRD): Subset qualifying for World Bank support.
  • Sub-Saharan Africa: Countries in Africa south of the Sahara desert.
  • Sub-Saharan Africa (IDA & IBRD countries): Subset of these countries eligible for development finance.
  • Sub-Saharan Africa (excluding high income): Same region excluding high-income nations (if any).
  • Upper middle income: Countries with GNI per capita between $4,256 and $13,845.
  • World: The entire global population.
